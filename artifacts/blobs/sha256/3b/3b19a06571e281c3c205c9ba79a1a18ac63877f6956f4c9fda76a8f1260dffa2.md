# Evaluation criteria

An attempt should be judged successful if it satisfies the following criteria:

- The attempt must implement a runnable PsyNet experiment from the prose prompt,
  not just a report or pseudocode.
- The experiment must run locally with PsyNet bot or simulated participants and
  must not use Prolific, Cint, AWS, paid recruitment, or production credentials.
- The attempt must include participant-facing evidence, preferably
  `participant.mp4` or equivalent browser/video evidence.
- The attempt must export local PsyNet data and include evidence of the export.
- The attempt must include an analysis script that runs on the exported or
  simulated PsyNet-format data.
- The attempt must include a short methods/results report that distinguishes
  actual PsyNet output from simulated participant behavior.
- The attempt must record provenance in `agent.json`, timeline events in
  `TIMELINE.md`, and reusable lessons in `LEARNINGS.md`.
- The attempt should not claim that LLM or bot participants reproduce human
  psychological behavior unless the evidence actually supports that claim.
