# Plan

## Science

This experiment will implement the core logic of Sanborn and Griffiths' Markov chain Monte Carlo with people method in a locally runnable PsyNet study. The scientific goal is to train participants on a one-dimensional category distribution for schematic fish height, then use their paired choices between a current chain state and a symmetric proposal to obtain samples from their subjective fish-farm category distribution. If participants approximately follow the Luce/Barker choice rule, the accepted chain states should recover the location and spread of the trained fish-farm distribution after burn-in.

## Methods

Participants will be assigned to one of four between-participant fish-farm conditions crossing the two Gaussian means, 3.66 cm and 4.72 cm, with the two Gaussian standard deviations, 0.13 cm and 0.31 cm. Ocean fish will be sampled uniformly from 2.63 cm to 5.76 cm. Fish will be rendered locally as inline SVG with a fixed length, color, eye, and tail; only body height will vary. The display will map centimeters to SVG units with a documented linear conversion, while all trial records preserve centimeter-valued heights.

The participant flow will begin with consent and translation-ready instructions explaining the ocean and fish-farm categories. The first task phase will be an initial feedback training block. Each training trial presents one fish sampled either from the ocean distribution or the participant's fish-farm distribution; the participant chooses "Ocean" or "Fish farm" and receives correctness feedback. The local test schedule will use shorter configurable block lengths, while constants in the experiment will also document a paper-like schedule of 120 initial training trials, alternating 60-trial MCMC and refresher blocks, and a 60-trial final no-feedback classification test.

The MCMC phase will interleave three within-participant chains. The chains will start from 2.63 cm, 4.20 cm, and 5.76 cm. On each MCMC trial, the experiment will choose one active chain, draw a proposal from a symmetric pseudo-Gaussian distribution centered on the chain's current height, reject exact-current proposals by resampling or by drawing from a zero-at-current discrete support, clamp proposals to a documented allowed height range, randomize current/proposal left-right position, and ask which fish is more likely to have come from the fish farm. Choosing the proposal updates that chain's state; choosing the current state leaves it unchanged. Refresher training blocks will appear between MCMC blocks, and the final no-feedback test block will measure category learning without feedback.

Every trial will record participant condition, block, trial type, trial index, reaction time, and display conversion constants. Training and test trials will record sampled category, sampled height, response, correctness, and feedback status. MCMC trials will record chain id, previous state, proposal state, accepted state, proposal acceptance, side assignment, selected side, proposal distribution parameters, and height bounds. These records should let a reviewer reconstruct each chain directly from exported trial data.

## Implementation

The experiment will live under `code/mcmc_with_people/` to avoid importing directly from a package named `code`. I will start from a minimal PsyNet experiment structure and include the usual local launch support files. Participant-facing text will use PsyNet's translator helper (`_ = get_translator()`) so strings can be extracted later.

For training and final classification trials, I will use `StaticTrial` / `StaticTrialMaker` subclasses with custom `show_trial`, `score_answer`, and `show_feedback` behavior. A shared fish rendering helper will produce local SVG markup from centimeter-valued heights. Training nodes will be generated from the participant's assigned condition; final test nodes will reuse the same rendering and scoring path but suppress feedback.

For the paired-choice Markov chains, I will use PsyNet's MCMCP architecture rather than manually post-processing independent choices. A custom `MCMCPNode` will hold the current fish height and chain metadata, create the overdispersed initial seeds, and generate symmetric proposals. A custom `MCMCPTrial` will render the current and proposed fish side by side, randomize their order, collect the selected side, and expose enough metadata for export. The MCMCP trial maker will use `chain_type="within"` and three chains per participant so each participant carries their own chain states through the experiment.

The timeline will be assembled from small helper functions: instructions, initial training, one or more configurable MCMC/refresher cycles, final no-feedback test, demographics or debrief where appropriate, and completion. The default development configuration will keep trial counts low enough for `psynet test local`, participant-flow recording, and performance checks; named constants will document how to switch to the paper-like schedule.

Bots will model approximate fish-farm preference with a noisy Gaussian likelihood rule. For training/test trials, bots will choose the more likely category stochastically according to the assigned condition. For MCMC trials, bots will choose between current and proposal with an exponentiated Luce/Barker rule so simulated chains recover condition differences. `test_check_bot` will assert that bots finish the planned phases, that MCMC trials update accepted states from prior chain state, and that exported metadata contains the reconstruction fields.

The analysis workflow will include a script or notebook under `evidence/analyses/` that reads exported data and a documented simulated dataset. It will plot three-chain traces by participant, apply a simple first-chain-crossing burn-in heuristic or a documented fallback, estimate recovered means and standard deviations from retained samples, compare estimates to assigned training parameters, report acceptance rates, and flag boundary hits. Simulated participants will cover all four conditions so the analysis can demonstrate recovery of mean and spread differences.

Before completing the attempt, I will run `python experiment.py`, `psynet test local`, `psynet simulate`, export data to `evidence/data.zip`, run the analysis notebook, collect Playwright/ffmpeg participant-flow evidence showing both training and MCMC trials, save a monitor snapshot, run `psynet performance-test local --n-bots 40 --duration-minutes 5 --time-factor 1.0`, and record any environment blockers honestly in `EVALUATION.md`.
